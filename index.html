F.A.Q

What game is this? A: It's a 2.5D narrative-based game where the beginning is the end, and the end is the beginning. Who is this game for? A: It's for children, young adults, and adults up to 40 years old who enjoy calm, narrative-driven indie games. Is this game played from right to left? Why? A: Yes, we wanted to showcase a different perspective of playing a platformer, taking time into account, hence the left-to-right gameplay. What is the reason for this type of narrative? And what is the narrative? A: The reason for this narrative is to convey to the player that time and the passage of time are things we must face and manage before they run out. Metaphorically, in the game, it involves the destruction of the sun or its approaching to Earth, where humans have "explored" manipulating time, and now only one person can control the event and prevent it (the game's main character). Can this game be played? Will it be available? A: Currently, only a demo is available to play, which can be found on Itch.io or Steam. When will it be released? At what price and on which platforms? A: There is no set release date yet, and the price is still under discussion. How do you play? A: Similar to a platformer, you will be able to move left and right and jump, but in our game, there will be a time mechanic to rewind certain parts or objects, also involving puzzle-solving. How was it made? Which engine and programs were used for this game? A: The game is being developed in Unreal Engine (v5.0), using programs like 3ds Max, Blender, FMOD, Reaper, Adobe Illustrator, and Photoshop. It was first conceptualized, then designed, and now being built.

Control Time A 2.5D side-scrolling, narrative-based game where the story will be revealed through objects and "marks" left behind. However, the player can control time to solve puzzles, and the beginning is the end, and the end is the beginning (left-to-right gameplay).
